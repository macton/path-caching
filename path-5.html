1. Step 0: Divide map into regions. e.g. grid or polygon groups.
  2. Step 1: Find all inner corner (edges) which can potentially occlude line of sight.
  3. Step 2: Calculate Definitely Visible Set (DVS) per regions. Regions which can be competely seen from every point in a region.
  4. Step 3: From DVS and inner corners, calculate portals per DVS. Edges/polys which can potentially lead to additional visible areas.
  5. Step 4: For any position, cast rays through portal edges/corners to walls, or through other portals (recursively.)
  6. Step 5: For any position, given complete visible area, find inner corners (where test point is behind at least one edge/poly of corner)
  7. Step 6: For any inner corner, gather DVS and portals from all regions which share that corner. Merge into a single set.
  8. Step 7: For any inner corner, cast rays through portals to find visible area. Collect any inner corner in light-of-sight. Take special care to add inner corners along boundary of merged DVSs.
